## 7. What You Got Right

We are not dismissing everything. Three points across your three rounds of feedback are valid and we've acted on them.

### Thought leadership / blog depth

You correctly identified that our AI narrative tends to stay product-level ("our AI does X") rather than expanding to broader industry perspective ("what AI means for pharmacy in 2028"). We've now built a full thought leadership and blog strategy covering four pillars (Industry Lens, Operational Thinking, AI in Pharmacy, Services & Clinical Practice), with a 2-post-per-month cadence, a blog-to-LinkedIn derivative workflow, AEO/search targets, and long-form voice rules. This is already committed to the repo.

### Pipeline attribution

Tracking demo requests and qualified leads from specific posts is a genuine operational gap. Our metrics track impressions, reactions, comments, reposts, and clicks — but not what happens after the click. This is a CRM and analytics issue rather than a content strategy issue, but it's a fair point.

### Engagement questions are underused

Only 2 engagement question posts out of 156 (1%). Our formula targets 1–2 per month. Posts like "Pick One Essentials" and "ChatGPT Prompts" both performed well. This is a genuine execution gap and April's plan corrects it.

## 8. What We'd Need From a Consultant

If we were to work with an external content consultant, we'd need someone who:

1. **Reads the strategy before critiquing the output.** Our content formula, voice guide, beat sequences, and anti-AI writing guide exist because we've already diagnosed and solved most of the problems you've raised. A consultant who recommends things that already exist hasn't done the preparation.

2. **Uses our data, not generic benchmarks.** We have performance data on 277 posts. We know which formats, types, and approaches drive engagement in UK pharmacy. Recommendations should build on that data, not override it with a template from a different market.

3. **Can write better than our current output, not worse.** Sample rewrites that would lower our engagement rates and attribute the wrong data to the wrong pharmacy do not demonstrate the expertise needed to improve our content.

4. **Understands the sector.** UK community pharmacy is a regulated, conservative, relationship-driven market. Content that works for horizontal SaaS does not automatically translate. Our audience of 5,700 followers includes pharmacy owners who make purchasing decisions for their businesses. Reach is less important than trust.
