## Slide 1 — Pharmacy First under pressure

Headline slide stating "Pharmacy First Under Pressure," illustrated with a hanging "Closed" sign, signalling concern that increasing pressures may force pharmacies to stop delivering the service.

---

## Slide 2 — New service requirements

Slide explaining that to receive monthly funding, pharmacies must now deliver Pharmacy First, contraception, and hypertension services, with ABPM becoming mandatory and failure to deliver meaning no payment.

---

## Slide 3 — Funding loss warning

Slide warning that missing required service delivery can result in losing up to £1,000 in monthly payment, with no partial credit if targets are not met.

---

## Slide 4 — Delivery gap

Slide stating that only around 30% of pharmacies deliver the full required service bundle, explaining ABPM is complex and time-consuming in busy walk-in pharmacy settings.

---

## Slide 5 — NHS dentistry comparison

Slide drawing comparison with dentistry reforms, noting dentists left NHS contracts when funding pressures increased, and warning pharmacy could face a similar situation.

---

## Slide 6 — Private vs NHS services

Slide presenting the growing question of whether pharmacies should focus more on private services where they control workflow and margins, illustrated with signposts pointing toward NHS and private options.

---

## Slide 7 — Future questions

Final slide asking what should change next, questioning whether ABPM should be replaced, funding made more flexible, or a new pharmacy model built, stating that pharmacy's future depends on what happens next.
