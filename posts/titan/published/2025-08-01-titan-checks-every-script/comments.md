Ruth Hulton I agree its complicated especially for Superintendent Pharmacists who are ultimately responsible. There are two important concepts that Titan AI tries to address as part of its design: Trust and Control.

Trust - That high risk meds are always reviewed by pharmacists every time. You can even set the meds, scenarios (eg new patients) and groups of patients (eg MDS) that you don't want AI to be applied to.

Titan AI is based on learning from pharmacists interventions on prescriptions. Anyone who wants to use Titan AI is encouraged to understand how it works, learns and applies its learning.
You can also run Titan AI in partial mode, where the AI makes changes but a pharmacist has to approve the changes.

Control - pharmacists are in control of how long the TITAN AI is applied for, typically 2-4 hours.
After each period pharmacists have to do a statistically significant sample audit before they can run the AI again. 

Measures like the above show the sophistication that Titan AI has and this is why hundreds of Titan customers are using it and achieving efficiencies without compromising patient safety.
