Slide explaining PMR systems present many metrics at once, listing examples such as prescription volumes, queue lengths, rejections, backlogs, safety alerts, stock shortages, and service counts, emphasising that more data does not always lead to better decisions.

## Slide 3 — What actually matters

Slide highlighting key day-to-day measures that matter most: time to dispense safely, service output performance, label accuracy, manageable team workload, and prescription collection rates.

## Slide 4 — What to stop worrying about

Slide listing metrics teams should stress less about, including scripts per hour without context, every individual EPS rejection, comparisons with large chain pharmacies, and reacting to daily data fluctuations rather than long-term trends.

## Slide 5 — What to focus on instead

Slide advising pharmacies to set goals based on real outcomes, use dashboards teams understand, allow automation to highlight key insights, and track performance weekly instead of minute-by-minute.
