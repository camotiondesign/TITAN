**Generated:** December 2025

# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This playbook distills patterns from your highest-performing case studies and LinkedIn campaigns. Key findings:

- **Written case studies** that perform best use a clear transformation arc: Challenge ‚Üí Solution ‚Üí Results, with emotional quotes and specific metrics
- **Video case studies** follow a 6-beat structure: Hook ‚Üí Character ‚Üí Challenge ‚Üí Solution ‚Üí Results ‚Üí CTA, averaging 2-3 minutes
- **LinkedIn carousels** drive 60.5% engagement (vs 5.8% for single images) and should be 50%+ of your content mix
- **151+ word captions** perform 2.5x better than short captions (33.2% vs 12.0% engagement)
- **7+ hashtags** drive 3x better performance than 1-3 hashtags (27.2% vs 8.4% engagement)

## 1.1 Priory Pharmacy Case Study Analysis

### Narrative Structure

**Opening (Paragraph 1):**
- **Location + Character:** "Located in the heart of Orpington, Priory Pharmacy is a modern, high-volume pharmacy..."
- **Credibility:** "Since taking over the business in 2021, pharmacist and owner Prab..."
- **Achievement:** "dispensing nearly 16,000 items every month without robotic automation or overtime"
- **Positioning:** "model for calm and scalable community pharmacy"

**Key Metrics Section:**
- Bullet format with bold numbers
- 3-4 key metrics maximum
- Mix of volume, efficiency, and capability metrics

**The Challenge:**
- **Time anchor:** "When Prab took over the pharmacy in 2021..."
- **Problem framing:** "Everything was manual, systems didn't talk to each other..."
- **Emotional quote:** "Five years ago, I would not be able to be having this conversation with you. I'd probably still be in my dispensary trying to check hundreds of baskets, being incredibly stressed and just not really enjoying pharmacy."
- **Stakes:** "Trying to scale that way wasn't just hard. It was a fast track to burnout."

**How They Did It (4 sections):**
1. **A PMR Built into the Blueprint** ‚Äî Decision + physical design
2. **Letting AI Handle the Routine** ‚Äî Automation + delegation
3. **Faster, Smarter Collections** ‚Äî Patient experience
4. **Prioritising Prescriptions** ‚Äî Workflow optimization

**The Results:**
- Bullet list of outcomes
- Mix quantitative and qualitative
- End with emotional quote: "At the moment, we're doing nearly 16,000 items, and we do this without a robot. There's no chaos. Everything's really calm and really organised."

**What's Next:**
- Forward-looking section (optional but effective)
- Shows evolution and future vision

**The Takeaway:**
- Philosophical conclusion
- Broader lesson beyond the case study
- Final quote that encapsulates the transformation

### Writing Patterns

**Sentence Structure:**
- **Short, punchy sentences** for emphasis: "Trying to scale that way wasn't just hard. It was a fast track to burnout."
- **Longer, descriptive sentences** for context: "Located in the heart of Orpington, Priory Pharmacy is a modern, high-volume pharmacy committed to delivering safe, efficient, and patient-first care."
- **Varied length** creates rhythm and prevents monotony

**Emotional Cues:**
- **Before state:** Stress, chaos, burnout, frustration
- **After state:** Calm, control, freedom, growth
- **Transformation language:** "transformed," "freed," "scaled," "designed"

**Detail Density:**
- **Specific numbers:** "16,000 items," "70% of scripts," "150+ baskets"
- **Specific features:** "five batch flow workstations," "Titan's Kanban board," "Titan Mobile device"
- **Specific outcomes:** "No late nights or overtime checking," "Live status updates"

**Pacing:**
- **Fast opening:** Gets to the point quickly
- **Slower middle:** Explores solutions in detail
- **Fast closing:** Quick results summary, then philosophical takeaway

### What Keeps Readers Engaged

1. **Emotional quotes** break up text and add authenticity
2. **Specific metrics** provide credibility
3. **Clear problem-solution structure** creates narrative tension
4. **Forward-looking sections** show evolution
5. **Philosophical takeaways** elevate beyond case study

## 1.4 Standardised Writing Framework for TITAN Written Case Studies

### Required Structure

#### 1. HEADLINE
**Format:** "How [Pharmacy Name] [Achievement] [Without/With] [Key Differentiator]"

**Examples:**
- "How Brighton Hill Pharmacy Scaled from 4K to 17K Without the Stress"
- "Scaling Safely Without Robots: Inside Priory Pharmacy's 16K-Item Workflow"
- "How BMP Scaled to 170,000 Items and 500 Percent Service Growth"

**Patterns:**
- Start with "How" for transformation stories
- Include specific numbers when possible
- Include "without" or "with" to show contrast
- Keep under 70 characters for SEO

#### 2. OPENING PARAGRAPH (100-150 words)

**Required elements:**
- **Location + Pharmacy name**
- **Character introduction** (owner/pharmacist name)
- **Current state achievement** (items/month, services, etc.)
- **Key differentiator** (calm, digital-first, service-led, etc.)
- **Positioning statement** (model, leader, example)

**Example structure:**
```
[Location], [Pharmacy Name] is a [descriptor] pharmacy [run/owned] by [name], [role]. 
[Since/When] [time anchor], [they] have [achievement]. 
[Key differentiator statement]. 
[Positioning statement].
```

#### 3. KEY METRICS SECTION

**Format:** Bullet list with 3-4 metrics

**Required metrics (choose based on story):**
- Items per month (if high-volume story)
- Time saved (hours/week or percentage)
- AI checking percentage
- Service growth (percentage or multiplier)
- Team size (if relevant)
- Paperless status (if relevant)

**Style:**
- Bold the numbers
- Keep descriptions short
- Mix quantitative and qualitative

#### 4. THE CHALLENGE (150-200 words)

**Required elements:**
- **Time anchor** ‚Äî "When [name] took over/started..."
- **Problem description** ‚Äî Manual, paper-heavy, stressful, unscalable
- **Emotional quote** ‚Äî First-person struggle quote
- **Stakes** ‚Äî What happens if they don't solve it

**Structure:**
```
[Time anchor], [problem description]. 
[Specific pain points]. 
[Emotional quote ‚Äî 1-2 sentences]. 
[Stakes ‚Äî what failure looks like].
```

#### 5. HOW THEY DID IT (4-6 sections, 200-300 words each)

**Required sections (choose 4-6 based on story):**

**A. Decision/Foundation**
- Why they chose Titan
- When they implemented
- Initial setup/design decisions
- Quote about decision clarity

**B. Core Feature 1 (AI, Batch Flow, Mobile, etc.)**
- How feature works in their context
- Specific workflow description
- Impact on daily operations
- Quote about feature value

**C. Core Feature 2**
- Same structure as above

**D. Workflow Design**
- Physical layout
- Process flow
- Team structure
- Quote about workflow

**E. Service Growth (if applicable)**
- Services launched
- Capacity increased
- Revenue impact
- Quote about services

**F. Team Empowerment (if applicable)**
- Staff upskilling
- Role changes
- Culture shift
- Quote about team

**Section structure:**
```
### [Number]. [Section Title]

[Context paragraph ‚Äî 2-3 sentences]. 

> "[Quote ‚Äî 1-2 sentences]"

[Impact paragraph ‚Äî 2-3 sentences explaining the quote's significance].
```

#### 6. THE RESULTS (100-150 words)

**Format:** Bullet list + summary paragraph

**Required elements:**
- 5-7 specific outcomes
- Mix quantitative and qualitative
- End with emotional quote

**Structure:**
```
[Bullet list of outcomes]

[Summary paragraph ‚Äî 2-3 sentences]. 

> "[Final quote ‚Äî 1-2 sentences]"
```

#### 7. THE TAKEAWAY (100-150 words)

**Required elements:**
- **Broader lesson** ‚Äî What this teaches about pharmacy/pharmacy systems
- **Philosophical statement** ‚Äî Deeper meaning
- **Final quote** ‚Äî Encapsulating quote
- **CTA** ‚Äî "üëâ Book a demo today" or "üëâ Read more case studies"

**Structure:**
```
[Name]'s journey shows [broader lesson]. 
[Philosophical statement about what's possible]. 

> "[Final quote ‚Äî 1-2 sentences]"

üëâ **[CTA]**
```

## 1.5 Style Guide for Tone, Language, and Clarity

### Tone

**Primary tone:** Professional, confident, empathetic

**Voice characteristics:**
- **Direct** ‚Äî No fluff, get to the point
- **Authentic** ‚Äî Real quotes, real struggles
- **Optimistic** ‚Äî Problems solved, not just described
- **Respectful** ‚Äî Honors pharmacist expertise
- **Forward-looking** ‚Äî Focuses on possibility, not limitations

### Language Patterns

**Use these words/phrases:**
- **Transformation:** "transformed," "redesigned," "built," "created"
- **Freedom:** "freed," "released," "enabled," "allowed"
- **Calm:** "calm," "organized," "smooth," "efficient"
- **Growth:** "scaled," "expanded," "grew," "increased"
- **Control:** "visibility," "clarity," "oversight," "tracking"

**Avoid these words/phrases:**
- **Generic:** "revolutionary," "game-changing," "disruptive" (unless in quote)
- **Negative:** "broken," "terrible," "awful" (use "challenging," "difficult")
- **Vague:** "better," "improved," "enhanced" (use specific metrics)
- **Jargon:** PMR-specific terms without explanation

### Sentence Structure

**Vary length:**
- **Short (5-10 words):** For emphasis, impact, transitions
- **Medium (15-25 words):** For description, context
- **Long (25+ words):** For complex ideas, detailed explanations

**Example:**
```
Prab's first decision was to install Titan. Everything changed. The team uses five batch flow workstations, allowing them to work on up to 150 baskets at once, with everything tracked in real time on Titan's Kanban board.
```

### Clarity Principles

1. **Lead with the outcome** ‚Äî "16,000 items without chaos" not "implemented Titan PMR"
2. **Use specific numbers** ‚Äî "70% of scripts" not "most scripts"
3. **Show, don't tell** ‚Äî "I can spend an hour with them and not be interrupted" not "Titan saves time"
4. **Explain technical terms** ‚Äî "Titan AI, which automates the bulk of clinical checking"
5. **Use active voice** ‚Äî "Prab transformed" not "Priory was transformed"

### Quote Integration

**Quote placement:**
- **After context** ‚Äî Set up the quote, then deliver it
- **Before explanation** ‚Äî Quote, then explain its significance
- **Standalone** ‚Äî Let powerful quotes stand alone

**Quote selection criteria:**
- **Emotional** ‚Äî Shows feeling, not just facts
- **Specific** ‚Äî Includes numbers or concrete details
- **Authentic** ‚Äî Sounds like a real person, not marketing copy
- **Actionable** ‚Äî Shows what's possible

**Quote formatting:**
- Use blockquotes (`>`) for longer quotes
- Use inline quotes for short phrases
- Always attribute quotes to the speaker

### Paragraph Structure

**Opening sentence:** Topic or transition  
**Middle sentences:** Development, examples, quotes  
**Closing sentence:** Impact or transition to next section

**Example:**
```
At the heart of the system is Titan AI, which automates the bulk of clinical checking. "The AI is currently checking and clearing about 70% of the prescriptions that we receive. So the volume that I need to review and check is really small." This intelligent delegation gives Prab the time to focus on services, prescribing, and leading the team.
```

## 2.1 Video Case Study Analysis

### Priory Pharmacy Longform Video Structure

**Total length:** ~2-3 minutes  
**Structure:** 6-beat narrative arc

**Beat 1: Hook (0:00-0:15)**
- **Content:** Character introduction + achievement statement
- **Quote:** "Meet Prab. When he's not picking up his niece, he's here in Orpington running Priory Pharmacy, and let's just say you can call him a smooth operator."
- **Visual:** Character introduction, pharmacy exterior
- **Purpose:** Establish character, credibility, achievement

**Beat 2: Challenge/Context (0:15-0:45)**
- **Content:** Problem framing + decision
- **Quote:** "We're doing nearly 16,000 items, and we do this without a robot, and there's no chaos. Everything's really calm and really organized."
- **Visual:** Dispensary shots, workflow demonstration
- **Purpose:** Show contrast, establish stakes

**Beat 3: Solution/Features (0:45-1:30)**
- **Content:** Core features + workflow
- **Quotes:** 
  - "I did not want to have any other PMR other than Titan"
  - "Titan AI and Batch Flow are my two modules that I love the most"
  - "The AI is currently checking and clearing about 70% of the prescriptions"
- **Visual:** Feature demonstrations, screen recordings, workflow shots
- **Purpose:** Show how it works, build credibility

**Beat 4: Impact/Transformation (1:30-2:00)**
- **Content:** Before/after contrast + emotional impact
- **Quote:** "Five years ago, I would not be able to be having this conversation with you. I'd probably still be in my dispensary trying to check hundreds of baskets, being incredibly stressed."
- **Visual:** Calm workflow, patient interactions, consultation room
- **Purpose:** Show transformation, emotional resonance

**Beat 5: Results/Proof (2:00-2:30)**
- **Content:** Specific outcomes + business impact
- **Quote:** "Thanks to Titan PMR, I'm probably still in community pharmacy. Otherwise I would have left a long time ago."
- **Visual:** Metrics on screen, team shots, success indicators
- **Purpose:** Prove the transformation, build trust

**Beat 6: CTA/Closing (2:30-3:00)**
- **Content:** Final statement + call to action
- **Quote:** "If someone was thinking about getting Titan, I would just tell them ‚Äî just sign straight away. Titan has given me my work life balance back."
- **Visual:** Final pharmacy shot, logo, CTA text
- **Purpose:** Inspire action, reinforce message

# 4. META-INSIGHT: TOP-PERFORMING LINKEDIN POSTS

## 4.1 What Hooks Consistently Perform Best

### Top Performing Hook Patterns

**1. POV Format (76.8% engagement)**
- **Pattern:** "POV: You [action]"
- **Example:** "POV: You process a prescription batch. One click..."
- **Why it works:** Creates immediate relatability, puts reader in the scenario
- **Use for:** Product features, workflow demonstrations

**2. Achievement Statement (65.1% engagement)**
- **Pattern:** "[Number] [Achievement] [Without/With] [Differentiator]"
- **Example:** "16K ITEMS. ZERO OVERTIME. NO CHAOS."
- **Why it works:** Specific, impressive, creates curiosity
- **Use for:** Case studies, transformation stories

**3. Double Question (58.0% engagement)**
- **Pattern:** "Still [problem]? Still [worry]?"
- **Example:** "Still manually checking every prescription? Still worried an error will slip through?"
- **Why it works:** Addresses pain points directly, creates tension
- **Use for:** Problem-solution content, feature highlights

**4. Contrast Statement (53.4% engagement)**
- **Pattern:** "[Not X]. [Y]."
- **Example:** "Your dispensary isn't slow. Your PMR is."
- **Why it works:** Reframes problem, creates "aha" moment
- **Use for:** Problem framing, positioning

**5. Transformation Statement (93.2% engagement)**
- **Pattern:** "How [Pharmacy] [Achievement] [Without/With] [Differentiator]"
- **Example:** "How one pharmacy scaled 3x without adding a single staff member."
- **Why it works:** Specific, achievable, creates curiosity
- **Use for:** Case studies, proof content
