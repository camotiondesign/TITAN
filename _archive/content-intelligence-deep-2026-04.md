## Q2: Doing Too Much / Not Enough / Content Gaps

### Doing Too Much Of

**1. Product feature single images without a problem hook (~30 posts, Jan–Mar 2026)**
Posts like "Filter out the noise," "Switch it on. Walk away," "Partial systems create full-scale problems" cluster in Jan–Feb 2026. Similar structure (problem → feature list → CTA), moderate performance (4–5% ER or zero notionsocial data). The issue isn't the topic — it's repetition without escalation.

**2. The "dispensing is not enough / one platform" frame on Titanverse (8+ posts)**
At least 8 Titanverse posts Oct–Dec 2025 make the same fragmentation argument without new evidence or a new angle. By post 5, the audience has heard it. Each repetition needs new proof (a new customer, a new data point, a new capability).

**3. Duplicate/variant posts to both brand accounts**
Multiple posts appear with identical captions on both titan and titanpmr accounts same day (TitanUp launch, SomeOfYouMissedIt, ScanMe). Creates data noise, dilutes page identity, confuses audience. ~30–40 of the 245 Titan posts are duplicates or near-variants.

**4. Generic "pharmacy is changing" editorial without new data**
Both brands have multiple posts asserting transformation without fresh evidence. High-level statements without specific proof underperform consistently.

### Not Doing Enough Of

**1. Named pharmacist video content**
The top-performing videos (BMP/Yusuf 11.5%, Sagar 7%, Tariq keynote 12.4%, Zad vox-pops 14.4%) are ALL real pharmacist interview/testimonial clips. These are rare (~15–20 in 245 posts). With 1,250+ Titan customers, the pipeline is unlimited. Should be at least one new named pharmacist video per fortnight.

**2. Multi-pharmacy group / head office stories**
Two HOD campaign posts address pharmacy group owners and superintendents (Sunil/Hollywood Chemist, Glen/26 pharmacies). This is an underserved audience segment. No more than 2–3 posts in this lane out of 245 total.

**3. Integration / partner posts**
PharmAppy/Priory Pharmacy video: **14.6% ER**, 666 impressions. The Priory repost generated one of the most substantive comment threads. Only ~5 partner-focused posts across 245 Titan posts.

**4. Structured community discussion posts**
One "You can only keep ONE Titan feature — drop your answer below" post exists. Low reach (notionsocial, 737 views) but the comment thread signals audience appetite. Never tested at scale with proper organic distribution.

### What Titanverse Does That Titan Doesn't

- **Product UI demo loops** — animated videos showing actual Titanverse interface (consultation flow, prescribing, booking). Titan almost never shows its UI in motion.
- **Clinical governance content** — GPhC inspector hook post, compliance module post. Addresses compliance anxiety directly.
- **Waitlist urgency** — every Titanverse post ends "Join the waiting list." Creates scarcity/exclusivity positioning.
- **Pop-culture meme format** — LOTR "One app to rule them all" (5.6% ER, 1,428 impressions). Titan has never tried this format.
- **Specific fragmentation angle** — "5 logins, multiple apps, chaos to clarity." More specific competitive frame than Titan's "dispensing is inefficient."

### What Titan Does That Titanverse Doesn't

- **Named customer case study campaigns** — 8–10 distinct named pharmacist campaigns with video + carousel + single image variants. Titanverse has 2 customer names (Yusuf/BMP, Rahul/Puri) with lighter treatment.
- **Industry advocacy** — bulk nomination, GTIN barcode, NHS commentary. Titanverse has one EPS nomination post and one dispensing-model editorial.
- **Relatable humour** — pharmacy battery meme, Valentine's parody, Christmas carousel, peer-to-peer meme.
- **Event live coverage** — Titan's Pharmacy Show cluster is its strongest single content burst. Titanverse benefited from the same event but produced fewer live posts.

### Where Titanverse Is Underperforming

1. **Raw reach** — 205 followers means most posts stay under 1,000–2,000 impressions. Primary lever: grow follower base via Titan PMR cross-promotion.
2. **Comment depth** — small recurrent audience (Hooman, Michael Holden, Abhyuday Daharwal). Growing but early.
3. **Named customer specificity** — "early adopters" as an unnamed group lacks the credibility of named pharmacist campaigns.
4. **Differentiation fatigue** — 8+ posts with "one platform, no fragmentation" frame without escalation. Repetition without new proof underperforms.

Zero posts on Mounjaro, semaglutide, or weight management clinic operations. Two-post series: "How Titan-using pharmacies are running weight management clinics profitably" + named pharmacist doing it.

**5. Titanverse needs 3–5 more named pharmacist case studies with Titan-level specificity**
"Rahul at BMP doubled their NMS consultations" — with real numbers, a face, a pharmacy name in the headline. Raw material exists in the interviews; it needs to be extracted and published as a campaign.

**6. Shift Titanverse's "one platform" posts to escalation mode — new evidence every time**
Each fragmentation post should introduce something new: a new customer voice, new capability, new data point. No more repeating the same frame without escalation.

**7. Cross-promote Titan → Titanverse systematically (1–2 posts per week)**
With 5,490 vs 205 followers, Titan PMR tagging Titanverse in natural posts is the single biggest follower-growth lever available. Hooman's comments on Titanverse posts demonstrate this already works.

**8. Prioritise Thursday for high-importance content**
Tariq keynote (Thu), Stop Nomination (Thu), Why IP Fails (Thu) — consistent pattern. Shift key posts from Mon–Wed to Thu and test.

**9. Create Scotland-specific content**
One comment surfaced the gap explicitly. A single Scotland-facing post would generate inbound from Scottish pharmacy and signal expansion intent.

**10. Stop cross-posting identical captions to both brand accounts**
~30–40 of Titan's 245 posts are duplicates or near-variants. Each page should post intentionally differentiated content. Where cross-posting is needed, differentiate caption angle or creative format.
