## Methodology Note

The posts.json files contain two data sources. Posts with `source: linkedin_api` have real engagement rates calculated from impressions (these are the reliable performance indicators). Posts with `source: notionsocial` have `engagement_rate: 0` and `impressions: 0` — but many still have reactions, views, and reposts recorded. For engagement rate analysis, this report uses the linkedin_api posts only (approximately 95 Titan posts and 40 Titanverse posts with real rates). For content pillar counting and thematic analysis, all 245 Titan and 50 Titanverse posts are used.

**Important caveat on engagement rates:** LinkedIn carousel CTRs are inflated by swipe-through actions counted as clicks. Posts with 60–90% "engagement rates" are almost entirely carousel swipe clicks, not link-outs or active intent. These are separated in the analysis where relevant.

## Q2: Too Much / Not Enough / Content Gaps

### Doing Too Much Of

**1. Duplicate posts / cross-posting the same content to both brand accounts**
The data contains many instances of identical captions posted across both brand=titan and brand=titanpmr slugs (e.g., the TitanUp 2026 launch posts on 2026-03-02 and 2026-02-27 appear twice — once with metrics, once with zero metrics). This inflates post count without adding audience value and creates confusion in the data. Estimated ~30–40 posts in Titan's 245 are duplicates/variants.

**2. Product feature single images with vague captions**
Posts like "Filter out the noise," "Switch it on. Walk away," "Don't rely on manual checking," and "Partial systems create full-scale problems" are competent but undifferentiated. They share similar structures (problem statement → feature list → CTA) and cluster in January–February 2026. Many have 0 engagement data (notionsocial source) or moderate performance (4–5% ER). There are roughly 30 of these feature-education posts in the Jan–Mar 2026 window alone.

**3. Generic "pharmacy is changing" editorial posts**
Posts asserting that "dispensing alone can't fund the future" or "pharmacy is shifting fast" appear across both brands repeatedly. While the argument is sound, the repetition without new evidence or specific angles dulls the impact. Both Titan and Titanverse have multiple posts using this frame without adding fresh data or perspectives.

### Not Doing Enough Of

**1. Named pharmacist case study video content**
The best-performing videos in the dataset are consistently the real pharmacist interview/testimonial clips: BMP (Yusuf) video at 11.5% ER with 1,779 views, Sagar at 7% with 828 views, Tariq keynote at 12.4% with 1,947 views. These are rare (roughly 15–20 long-form testimonial videos across 245 posts). The audience clearly values hearing real pharmacists speak.

**2. Multi-pharmacy group / head office stories**
There are two HEAD OFFICE campaign posts (Sunil at Hollywood Chemist operating without double cover; Glen at Knights running 26 pharmacies) that address a specific, underserved audience: pharmacy group owners and superintendents. These appear underweighted relative to independent single-pharmacy stories.

**3. Engagement-prompt posts (comment bait with genuine substance)**
The one direct engagement post in the dataset — "You can only keep ONE Titan feature: Batch, AI, Mobile, Repeat. Drop your answer below" — got only 3 reactions and 1 comment on 737 views (notionsocial data, likely not widely distributed). But audience interest signals from comments on other posts (Hooman's annotations, Kirit Shah on regulation, Scotland questions) show there is appetite for discussion. Titan has not tested structured community discussion posts at scale.

**4. Integration / partner announcement posts**
Posts about PharmAppy, DemDx, Ryft, IPCN partnerships tend to perform well: the PharmAppy/Priory Pharmacy video got 14.6% ER on 666 impressions. The Priory repost generated one of the most substantive comment threads (Hooman, Yusuf, pharmacy owners). There are only ~5 partner-focused posts across 245 Titan posts.

### Obvious Content Gaps

**Weight loss / GLP-1 (Mounjaro/semaglutide):** Zero posts on this topic in the Titan dataset. The weight management pharmacy market is the most commercially active private services area in UK pharmacy in 2025–2026. Titan's Pharmacy First and services content doesn't address it.

**NHS contract / funding news response:** Only one post (£158m clawback carousel) directly responds to a specific NHS financial event. When funding news breaks, Titan has not been producing rapid-response content that pharmacy owners would share.

**Specific Pharmacy First data posts:** Despite Pharmacy First being the central NHS services programme, Titan has no posts with Pharmacy First consultation volume data, earnings-per-consultation breakdowns, or case studies of pharmacies maximising Pharmacy First through freed-up pharmacist time.

**Staff wellbeing / burnout (substantive, not just memes):** The pharmacist battery meme reached 5,747 impressions organically — Titan's widest non-boosted reach in the set. The underlying topic (pharmacist burnout and wellbeing) is never addressed in a substantive long-form post.

## Immediate Recommendations

**1. Make named pharmacist video the consistent heartbeat of Titan's content.**
The data is unambiguous: Yusuf (BMP), Sagar (Brighton Hill), Asif (Safy's), and Tariq's keynote are the best-performing posts in the entire dataset on organic video metrics. There should be at least one new named pharmacist short-form video per fortnight. The pipeline of 1,250+ Titan customers provides unlimited source material.

**2. Build a Pharmacy Show-equivalent content cluster around TitanUp 2026 (June 7).**
The October Pharmacy Show cluster accounted for roughly 40% of Titan's highest-engagement posts. TitanUp 2026 should have the same pre-event build-up (countdown posts), live coverage (day-of videos, Zad-style vox pops, Tariq talk), and post-event recap (photo album, montage). The TitanUp hard-launch carousel and teaser posts (Feb–Mar 2026) show early momentum but the campaign needs the full treatment.

**3. Stop auto-renomination and GTIN/patient safety advocacy must continue — these are Titan's highest-reach organic posts.**
The GTIN barcode post reached 7,338 impressions — the widest organic reach in the dataset — and the Stop Nomination Ping-Pong post got 14 reposts (the highest in the dataset). These posts extend reach beyond Titan's follower base because pharmacists share them. There should be at least two advocacy posts per month during active policy windows. Link them to petitions, blog pieces, and responses from external organisations.

**4. Create content specifically for the Mounjaro/weight loss service gap.**
This is the biggest commercial white space in the dataset. UK community pharmacy's biggest private services opportunity in 2025–2026 has zero coverage in Titan or Titanverse's LinkedIn presence. A two-post mini-series ("How Titan-using pharmacies are running weight management clinics profitably" + a named pharmacist doing it) would address a live search intent.

**5. Titanverse needs 3–5 more named pharmacist case studies with the same specificity Titan has for its PMR customers.**
"Early adopters" as an anonymous group lacks the credibility of "Asif at Safy's Chemist, 97% fewer near misses." Titanverse needs posts that say "Rahul at BMP doubled their NMS consultations" and "Yusuf's 12-branch group centralised services delivery to one pharmacist" — with real numbers, faces, and pharmacy names in the headline. These already exist as raw material in the captions but aren't being amplified as a named case study campaign.

**6. Consolidate the "dispensing is not enough / one platform" messaging on Titanverse — it has been repeated too many times without escalation.**
At least 8 Titanverse posts between October and December 2025 make the same "too many apps, one platform is the answer" argument without adding new evidence or a new angle. Each post should either introduce a new pain point, a new customer voice, or a new product capability. The fragmentation observation post (reactions: 14, views: 656) underperformed exactly because it repeated a frame that had already been used 4 times.

**7. Grow Titanverse's follower base via cross-promotion and Titan page amplification — this is the single biggest lever.**
With only 205 followers, Titanverse posts are reaching a tiny audience regardless of content quality. Titan PMR has 5,490 followers. A systematic approach of Titan PMR commenting on and resharing selected Titanverse posts (Hooman's comments on Titanverse posts demonstrate this works) would accelerate follower growth. Specifically: Titan PMR should tag Titanverse in 1–2 posts per week where the connection is natural.

**8. Use Thursday as the primary posting day for high-importance content.**
The data (while not statistically definitive) shows Thursday generating several of Titan's highest-engagement non-Pharmacy-Show posts: Tariq keynote (12.4%), Stop Nomination Ping-Pong (8.7%), Why IP Fails (7.2%). Monday–Wednesday is currently the heaviest publishing day. Experiment with shifting key posts to Thursday and tracking whether this pattern holds.

**9. Develop a Scotland / devolved pharmacy content stream.**
Mohammed Adill Sheikh's comment ("No plans for Scotland yet?") on the IP Fails post is the only geographic signal in the comments data. If Titan serves Scottish pharmacies, a single Scotland-specific post (e.g., "Community Pharmacy Scotland — how Titan customers are navigating the different contract landscape") would generate inbound from the Scottish pharmacy community and signal expansion intent.

**10. Stop posting duplicate/variant content to both the titan and titanpmr brand accounts without meaningful differentiation.**
Multiple posts appear in the dataset with identical captions posted to both accounts on the same date (TitanUp launch, SomeOfYouMissedIt, ScanMe). These duplicate posts create data noise, dilute page identity, and confuse the audience. Each brand account should post content intentionally created for that page. Where cross-posting makes sense (e.g., TitanUp announcement to both), differentiate the caption angle or the creative format.
