# Titan PMR — Patterns From Winners

_Built from analysis of top 20 Titan PMR LinkedIn posts by engagement rate (min 500 impressions). Last updated: 2026-04-06._

---

## 1. HOOK PATTERNS — Exactly How Top Posts Begin

### Pattern A: Direct Observation / Scene-Setting (Most Common — ~40% of top 20)

Stark, observable reality. No warmup. Immediately grounded in a specific scenario.

**Real examples:**
- "This is what a modern pharmacy looks like."
- "While Rahul's pharmacy ran on paper and post-its."
- "If you want your pharmacy to grow, you can't rely on old systems."
- "Pharmacies are losing up to 3 hours a day"
- "The UK's most efficient pharmacies aren't working harder."
- "Working in pharmacy? Then you've definitely heard one of these."
- "Some pharmacies might walk away from Pharmacy First"
- "Pharmacy is broken."

**Structure:** Noun or observation. Present tense or past contrast. No preamble.

---

### Pattern B: Contrarian / Problem Reframe (~25%)

Challenges an assumption or reframes what the real problem is.

**Real examples:**
- "Pharmacy is broken. But it's not pharmacists that need fixing. It's the systems around them."
- "Most PMRs react to change. The best ones create it."
- "Pharmacy didn't need another PMR. It needed a new way of working."

**Structure:** State the accepted problem → flip it to the real cause. Two sentences max.

---

### Pattern C: Emotional / Relatable Opener (~20%)

Human, moment-based. Invites reader into a shared experience with low friction.

**Real examples:**
- "What a weekend. ❤️"
- "Some of you missed it. Here's your chance. 👀"
- "Inside Tariq's Pharmacy Show Talk. The message was clear."
- "Sometimes pharmacy is serious. It's good to laugh at it sometimes too 😂"

**Structure:** Short reaction or scene. Often uses a single emoji to set tone. Works especially for event posts and cultural content.

---

### Pattern D: Stat / Shock Opener (Less Common, High Ceiling)

Emoji + number/outcome + immediate context. Creates curiosity about the mechanism.

**Real examples:**
- "💥 Mounjaro prices are up 170% and pharmacies are feeling it!"
- "🚀 How Sagar 4x Pharmacy Items and spends 80% of his time on services"

**Structure:** Emoji + specific number + pharmacist reaction. Only works when the number is genuinely striking.

---

### What Is NEVER a Hook

- Abstract philosophical openings ("In today's rapidly evolving healthcare landscape...")
- Generic questions not grounded in a specific scenario
- Hashtags or mentions in the first line
- Corporate hedging ("We're pleased to announce...", "We're excited to share...")
- "Exciting news:" as an opener

## 2. BODY STRUCTURE — The Winning Flow

After the hook, top posts follow this arc:

```
Hook (1-2 lines)
    ↓
Specific detail or example (2-3 lines)
    ↓
Breakdown / Mechanism (3-5 sections, scannable)
    ↓
Emotional validation line (1-2 lines)
    ↓
Data point or direct quote (optional, if available)
    ↓
CTA (1-2 lines)
```

**Examples of the specific detail section:**
- "We joined Sunil Chandarana and the team at May + Thomson Pharmacy in Woking"
- "When Sagar took over Brighton Hill Pharmacy, it was doing 4,000 items a month"
- "From 1 September, Mounjaro's list price jumps by up to 170%"

**Examples of the breakdown section (bulleted, scannable):**
```
We're still being told:
• "You just count pills"
• "Why does it take more than 2 minutes?"
• "Aren't you basically a shop?"

Meanwhile, community pharmacies are:
• Delivering NHS-funded services
• Managing long-term conditions
• Keeping people out of GP waiting rooms
```

**Emotional validation examples:**
- "No chaos. No compromise."
- "This isn't about working harder. It's about what your system is doing quietly in the background."
- "It's not because he got lucky."

---

### Caption Length

| Type | Word Count | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Short engagement posts | 80–150 words | Emotional, event-based, cultural |
| Medium (sweet spot) | 150–250 words | Most top performers |
| Long case studies | 250–350 words | Heavy white space, emojis as separators |

**Never:** More than 400 words. If you have more to say, make a carousel or link to the full piece.

---

### Line Break Strategy

- Frequent paragraph breaks — every 1–3 sentences
- Creates visual rhythm that prevents wall-of-text
- Emojis used as visual separators for concepts, not decoration:
  - 📦 = inventory / items
  - 🧠 = AI / intelligence
  - ⏱️ = time saved
  - 🚀 = growth / speed
  - 💬 = engagement / conversation
- Never: 🎉🎉🎉 or stacked emoji

---

### Sentence Structure

- Average 8.4 words per sentence
- Fragments are encouraged: "No chaos. No overtime. No dispensary firefighting."
- Rare use of complex dependent clauses
- Second sentence often repeats or emphasises the hook

## 3. CTA PATTERNS — How Top Posts End

### Type 1: Link + Context (Most Common)
```
📖 Read the full case study: bit.ly/40r6NI4
📖 Read Rahul's full case study here → bit.ly/408lMqm
```
Emoji + short imperative + link. Immediately useful, no friction.

### Type 2: Product / Action
```
👉 titanpmr.com/join-us
👉 Book a demo at titanpmr.com
Register now: titanup.club
```
Arrow emoji + simple verb + URL. Single action only.

### Type 3: Engagement Question
```
💬 What would you do with 80% of your time back?
💡 What's one outdated pharmacy process you'd love to see disappear? Drop it in the comments. 👇
👀 Slide through — and share the worst myth you've heard in the comments
```
Open-ended question directly related to the post topic. Not "like if you agree."

### Type 4: Soft / Implied CTA
```
📍 Stand D55 | The Pharmacy Show
Until next year 👋
```
Contextual. Respects reader intelligence. Used for events and community posts.

### Type 5: No CTA (Cultural / Emotional Posts Only)
Posts about shared experience or gratitude don't ask for anything. The brand visibility IS the goal.

---

### What NEVER Appears in a CTA

- "Link in bio"
- "Drop a comment for more"
- "Tag someone who needs to see this"
- Multiple competing CTAs
- Lengthy CTAs (>2 lines)
- Hashtag-based engagement prompts

---

## 4. WHAT'S NEVER IN TOP POSTS

### Structural
- Long hashtag lists (top posts use 3–8, never 15+)
- Multiple paragraph breaks with no content between them
- Numbered lists without emojis unless showing a clear sequence
- All-caps for emphasis mid-caption

### Tonal / Language
- "We're proud to announce..." / "I'm excited to share..."
- "As you know..." / "As we discussed..."
- Abstract nouns without examples: "innovation," "transformation," "growth" used alone
- Inspirational platitudes: "The future is bright," "Together we can," "Be the change"
- Hedging language: "We believe," "We think," "We hope"
- Jargon without translation: "stakeholder engagement," "value-add," "synergies"
- Corporate politeness: "We appreciate your feedback"

### Specific Words That Don't Appear
- "Unfortunately" (as opener)
- "Exciting news" (just announce it)
- "Industry-leading," "best-in-class"
- "Utilize," "leverage" (as verb), "impact" (as verb)
- "Regarding," "pertaining to"

## 5. VOICE FINGERPRINT — 8 Observable Traits

### 1. The "No X. No Y. No Z." Structure (Signature)

Appears in ~40% of top posts. Creates rhythm, builds emphasis through repetition.

**Real examples:**
- "No chaos. No overtime. No dispensary firefighting."
- "No chaos. No compromise."
- "No flexibility. No partial credit. No margin for real-world pharmacy."
- "No paper. No panic. No wasted time."

The pattern implies: "The old way had X. We fixed it." Never states this explicitly.

---

### 2. Every Claim Is Grounded in Real Pharmacies

Not: "Many pharmacies struggle with X."
But: "When Sagar took over Brighton Hill Pharmacy, it was doing 4,000 items a month."

**Pattern:** Name → Location → Specific number → What changed.

---

### 3. Specificity Over Abstraction

| Instead of | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| "Modern pharmacy is complex" | "📦 Prabjaudt now safely runs 16,000 items a month" |
| "Services are important" | "⏱️ 20 pharmacist hours a week saved. Those hours now go into Pharmacy First, weight loss and medical cannabis clinics." |
| "Significant growth" | "17,000 items. 4x growth. 80% of time on services." |

---

### 4. Humanising Through Contradiction

Show people in before-and-after states. Not to shame the "before" — to make the shift feel earned and real.

**Pattern:** "[Name] [was in situation X]. [Something changed]. Fast forward to today."

**Real examples:**
- "Rahul's pharmacy ran on paper and post-its. The health hub vision was on hold... Fast forward to today."
- "Gareth spent years in senior roles across large organisations, but something was missing... So he made the move."
- "Yusuf knew BMP HEALTHCARE LTD could not keep absorbing that pressure... Then he saw Titan at the Pharmacy Show."

---

### 5. Authority Through Listening, Not Lecturing

Never: "We know better."
Always: "Here's what the profession told us."

**Real examples:**
- "We've spoken to pharmacists and dispensers in 2,000-item independents and 15,000+ item sites."
- "We asked the profession. Here's what we heard..."
- "From TITAN's survey of 95 pharmacies..."
- "At The Pharmacy Show 2025, Tariq Muhammad shared what many in the room already felt."

---

### 6. Naming Real People and Real Barriers

Not: "Some pharmacies struggle with walk-in models."
But: "Only 30% of pharmacies currently meet the full bundle. ABPM is slow, low demand, and hard to convert in a walk-in model."

This is diagnostic writing. It names the actual barrier — not the symptom.

---

### 7. Tension → Release Structure

Nearly every top post creates a problem space, then resolves it:

- Problem: "Pharmacy is broken."
- Release: "But it's not pharmacists that need fixing. It's the systems around them."

- Problem: "Mounjaro prices are up 170% and pharmacies are feeling it!"
- Release: "Within days, Titan PMR launched a custom reporting suite..."

This creates a narrative arc in 200 words. Reader feels the tension, then relief, then confidence.

---

### 8. Italics for Direct Quotes (Sparingly)

Used only for verbatim quotes or specific key phrases. Creates a "voice within the voice" — you hear the pharmacist, not Titan's interpretation.

**Examples:**
- 𝘐 𝘢𝘮 𝘯𝘰𝘵 𝘨𝘭𝘶𝘦𝘥 𝘵𝘰 𝘵𝘩𝘦 𝘣𝘦𝘯𝘤𝘩. 𝘞𝘦 𝘢𝘳𝘦 𝘯𝘰𝘸 𝘢 𝘤𝘰𝘮𝘮𝘶𝘯𝘪𝘵𝘺 𝘩𝘦𝘢𝘭𝘵𝘩 𝘩𝘶𝘣.
- Never for random emphasis. One quote per post maximum.

## 6. SINGLE IMAGE FORMULA

### What Works

| Rank | Type | Hook | Why It Works |
|------|------|------|-------------|
| 1 | Real event photo | "What a weekend. ❤️" | Personal, moment-based. Image does the heavy lifting. |
| 2 | Tour / insider access | "If you want to see the future of pharmacy ✨" | Travelogue energy. Reads like insider access, not an ad. |
| 3 | Symbolic metaphor | "Baskets down. Auto-print defeated. ✨" | Poetic. 23 words. Visual metaphor. |
| 4 | Before/After messaging | "Pharmacy didn't need another PMR." | Clear transition. Education in one screen. |
| 5 | Ethical/advocacy | "The patient comes first. Stop nomination ping-pong." | Stakes are clear. Petition CTA. |

**Single image caption rule:** The image must do the heavy lifting. Caption adds context, feeling, or story — not description of what's in the image.

## 7. CAROUSEL FORMULA

### Core Structure (Applies to All Carousel Types)

```
Slide 1: Hook + Question or Setup
Slides 2–4: Proof / Breakdown / Mechanism (one idea per slide)
Slides 5–7: Outcome / Impact / Direct Quote
Final slide: CTA (link, question, or soft invite)
```

No filler slides. Every slide must move the story forward.

---

### Carousel Types by Performance

**Type A: Case Study (Highest Engagement — avg 85–99%)**
Structure: Before → What Changed → Outcomes → Quote → Invite
Examples: Sagar 4x growth, Rahul health hub, Priory Pharmacy
Key: Real person + real numbers + direct quote. Never fabricated.

**Type B: Myth-Busting / Education (avg ~109%)**
Structure: "You've heard..." → Common myths → What's actually true → Proof → Link
Key: Resonates with professional frustration. Validates the reader's lived experience.

**Type C: Policy / Industry Change (avg 96–104%)**
Structure: "What's Changing" → Timeline → Specific impacts → How to prepare → Resources
Key: Diagnostic of real pain points. Written like a briefing, not a blog post.

**Type D: Cultural / Humour (avg 81–91%)**
Structure: Relatable scenario → Escalation → Resonance moment
Key: Zero product messaging. Just shared experience. Makes brand feel human.

**Type E: Team / Behind-the-Scenes (avg 74–79%)**
Structure: Context → Experience → Gratitude → Invitation for next interaction
Key: Celebrates people in the ecosystem, not the product.

---

### Carousel Visual Principles (Reflected in Captions)

- Each slide is self-contained — can read one slide + caption section and understand the point
- Emoji use in caption is strategic, not decorative
- Design consistency assumed (not described in caption)
- No "swipe to learn more" teases — every slide earns attention

---

## 8. THE 10-POINT WINNING FORMULA

In priority order:

1. **Specific Before → After that resonates with a pain the target pharmacist feels today**
2. **Name real people. Give real numbers. Quote them directly.**
3. **Use the "No X. No Y. No Z." tension-release or equivalent**
4. **Short sentences. Fragments. Rhythm over perfection.**
5. **One emoji per concept — never decorative**
6. **Breakdown: 4–6 bullet points max, each a single idea**
7. **Emotional validation line: "No chaos," "Just a calm," "This isn't about working harder"**
8. **CTA that matches post type: link if educating, demo if promoting, question if engaging**
9. **Avoid corporate language entirely. Write like you're in a pharmacy, not a boardroom.**
10. **If it's a carousel: no filler slides. If it's a single image: let the image do the heavy lifting.**

## 9. INSIDE VOICE vs. OUTSIDE VOICE — The Master Principle

_Added 2026-04-07. This is the single most important distinction in Titan's content quality._

### The Problem

When Titan's content performs worst, it's because the writing describes Titan from the outside. When it performs best, the writing is already inside the thing — inside the pharmacy, inside the belief, inside the movement.

This is what "brand awareness" actually means for Titan. Not reach. Not recognition. **Content that IS Titan rather than content that TALKS ABOUT Titan.**

---

### Inside Voice — What It Sounds Like

The writer is already in the room. They're reporting from inside the experience.

**Real examples from top-performing posts:**
- *"Krishna peeled the sticker this week. Put it straight on the window."* — You're there. Present tense. Specific action.
- *"Prabjaudt now runs 16,000 items a month. He'd need 3 or 4 extra staff to do the same volume without Titan."* — Inside the pharmacy's reality.
- *"5 years ago, Prabjaudt was ready to leave pharmacy. Now he runs 16,000 items a month. No overtime. No burnout."* — Inside his life, not describing his life.
- *"The nomination ping-pong stops here."* — Takes a position. Insider frustration, not explanation.
- *"Checks every script. Every time. No exceptions."* — Titan's conviction stated as a fact, not a feature.

**Characteristics of inside voice:**
- Present tense or specific past tense (this happened, not "this is the kind of thing that happens")
- Names the pharmacy, names the person, names the number
- Takes positions — doesn't explain both sides
- Uses pharmacy language naturally (bench, baskets, owings, dispensary)
- Reads like the author was in the room

---

### Outside Voice — What to Avoid

The writer is explaining Titan to an observer. They're describing from a distance.

**Signs you've drifted to outside voice:**
- "This is what modern pharmacy looks like." (describing a concept, not a moment)
- "This moment captures what going live with Titan really means." (explaining the significance instead of showing it)
- "Titan is helping pharmacies do X." (Titan as subject, not the pharmacist)
- "As you know, the pharmacy landscape is evolving..." (editorial preamble — instant disqualifier)
- "We're proud to support the profession." (corporate distance)
- Writing about pharmacy from the perspective of a healthcare journalist, not a pharmacy owner

**Why it fails:** Readers feel the distance. They're being told about something rather than let into something. Brand awareness collapses to brand description.

---

### The Test

Read your caption draft. Ask: **Am I in the room, or am I describing the room?**

If you're describing: rewrite from inside. Find the specific moment, the specific person, the specific number. Drop into the experience. Take a position.

---

### Why This Matters for Brand Awareness

Brand awareness for Titan isn't reach or impressions. It's the moment a pharmacist reads a caption and already knows who wrote it before they see the logo.

The voice, the values, the belief system — all present in the writing itself. That's what the top 20 posts have. That's what the editorial drift lost.

When Titan was "smashing it," every post was inside the pharmacy, inside the conviction, inside the movement. The reader felt like they were being let in on something. That's brand awareness.

When content drifts to editorial/observational, the brand disappears — replaced by generic industry commentary that any PMR company could have written.

**Rule:** If another PMR company could have written this caption, rewrite it.
