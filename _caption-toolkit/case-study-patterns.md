# Case Study Patterns — Interview to Post

_Built from analysis of Prab, Sagar, Yusuf, Pritee, Rahul interviews + resulting LinkedIn posts. Last updated: 2026-04-06._

---

## The Core Formula: 6-Step Transformation Arc

Every high-performing case study post follows this arc — whether it's a 200-word single image caption or a 7-slide carousel.

```
Step 1: HOOK — The tension or outcome (before or after state)
Step 2: BEFORE — The situation before Titan. Name the specific pain.
Step 3: DECISION — The moment something changed. What triggered the switch?
Step 4: AFTER — Concrete outcomes. Numbers. Named services. Time gained.
Step 5: INSIGHT — The larger meaning. What does this prove about pharmacy?
Step 6: CTA — Read more / book a demo / conversation question
```

**Important:** Steps 4 and 5 can be reordered. Sometimes the insight lands harder when it comes before the data.

## The 7 Types of Extractable Moments (What to Listen For)

When reading or listening to an interview, these are the moments worth capturing:

### 1. The Tipping Point
The specific moment they decided to change. Not the general dissatisfaction — the actual event.

**Signal phrases:**
- "I was fed up with..."
- "One day we had..."
- "I saw Titan at the Pharmacy Show and..."
- "After [specific event], I knew something had to change."

**Why it matters:** This is always the emotional hook. It's the moment the audience recognises themselves.

---

### 2. The Before Numbers
What were they actually running before? Items per month, hours lost, paper process, staff count.

**Signal phrases:**
- "We were doing about X items..."
- "I was spending X hours on..."
- "We had X full-time staff just doing..."
- "Every day we were manually..."

**Why it matters:** Without a before number, the after number means nothing. The contrast is the story.

---

### 3. The After Numbers
The measurable outcomes post-Titan. Be precise — don't round off if you don't need to.

**Signal phrases:**
- "We're now at X items..."
- "We've saved X hours per week..."
- "We've added X services..."
- "Dispensing errors are down X%..."

**Why it matters:** This is what gets shared. Specific numbers get quoted. "Significant improvement" does not.

---

### 4. The Specific Capability That Changed Everything
Not the general system — one specific feature or function that unlocked the transformation.

**Signal phrases:**
- "The game-changer was..."
- "What I didn't expect was..."
- "The AI transcription / EPS / reporting suite..."
- "The thing that freed us up most was..."

**Why it matters:** Creates product credibility without being an ad. "We got Titan" is weak. "The auto-print elimination meant..." is specific.

---

### 5. The Emotional Shift
How did they feel before vs after? What can they now do that they couldn't?

**Signal phrases:**
- "I can actually focus on..."
- "I'm not glued to the bench anymore."
- "For the first time, I feel like..."
- "My staff aren't burning out."
- "I could finally take a weekend off."

**Why it matters:** This is the most shareable line. It's human. It's what the audience wants to feel.

---

### 6. The Broader Vision
Where are they going? What's possible now that wasn't before?

**Signal phrases:**
- "We want to become a health hub..."
- "We're looking at adding..."
- "The plan is to open a second branch..."
- "Now that we're not drowning in admin, we can..."

**Why it matters:** Adds aspiration. Signals that the transformation is ongoing. Titan becomes part of a bigger ambition, not just a tool swap.

---

### 7. The Quote Worth Stealing
One sentence that is so specific and so personal that it could only have come from this pharmacist.

**Examples from real interviews:**
- "I am not glued to the bench. We are now a community health hub." — Rahul
- "I can't imagine going back to the way it was." — Sagar
- "Every single item used to be a potential error. Now I trust the system." — Yusuf
- [Record verbatim, do not paraphrase]

**Why it matters:** Direct quotes, rendered in italics, are the most trusted signal in a caption. It says: this is a real person who said this, not a brand claim.

## What Gets Left Behind (And Why)

Not everything from an interview becomes a post. What stays on the cutting room floor:

| Content | Reason Not Used |
|---------|----------------|
| Vague praise ("Titan is great") | No specificity — could be any product |
| Process descriptions without outcomes | Tells readers what happened, not what changed |
| Technical feature discussion | Audience isn't technical — lead with outcome, not mechanism |
| Anything about implementation difficulties | Risks creating doubt. Only include if there's a resolution. |
| Comparisons to named competitors | Titan doesn't name other PMRs. Ever. |
| Grievances about the NHS system (without resolution) | Creates negativity with no payoff |
| Anything that contradicts published post | If Sagar said X in the interview but the LinkedIn post says Y, post wins. |

## Strategic Metrics Placement Guide

Where and how numbers appear in the caption matters as much as the number itself.

**Rule 1: Lead with impact, not mechanism**
- ❌ "Titan's auto-print elimination system removed paper from the workflow, which then freed up..."
- ✅ "20 pharmacist hours a week. Gone. Those hours now go into Pharmacy First."

**Rule 2: Name the service the time goes to**
- ❌ "More time for services."
- ✅ "More time for Pharmacy First, weight management, and their new medical cannabis clinic."

**Rule 3: Use the contrast format**
- "4,000 items a month → 17,000 items a month. Same team. Same building."
- "80% of Sagar's time used to go on dispensing. Now it goes on services."

**Rule 4: Round numbers feel fabricated — specific numbers feel earned**
- ❌ "saved roughly 20 hours"
- ✅ "saved 22 pharmacist hours per week"

**Rule 5: Metrics appear in the body, never the hook**
Unless it's a stat-first hook (Pattern D). In all other hook types, the number comes after context has been established.

## The 8-Signal Interview Scoring System

When reading a new interview, score it to know how much content it can generate.

### Tier 1 Signals (Each worth 3 posts)
- Clear before/after numbers (items, hours, errors)
- Named specific services added post-Titan
- Emotional quote that could only come from this person
- A "tipping point" moment — specific, personal, visual

### Tier 2 Signals (Each worth 1–2 posts)
- Broader vision / growth ambition
- Specific capability that unlocked the change
- Challenge they overcame during implementation
- Comparison to old system (named without competitor-naming)

### Tier 3 Signals (Good for context, not standalone posts)
- General positive sentiment
- Feature mentions without outcomes
- Vague future aspirations

**Scoring guide:**
- 3+ Tier 1 signals: Full case study series (5–8 posts across formats)
- 1–2 Tier 1 signals: 2–3 posts (one carousel + one or two singles)
- 0 Tier 1 signals: Supporting content only — quote post, short caption, brand mention
