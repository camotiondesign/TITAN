- 2025/26 NHS funding uplift and Pharmacy First funding secured

What Titanverse is NOT in this content: a scribe, an AI assistant, a smart notepad. It is infrastructure. The difference matters in every line.

## Carousel 2 — What Running 300 NMS Actually Takes

**Content type:** Mechanism / Operating Artefact
**Post day:** Wednesday
**Hook logic:** Big number → why it's hard → system failure at scale → infrastructure reveal

---

### Slides

**Slide 1 — The problem**
30 NMS consultations a month is manageable.
300 is a different operation entirely.
Most pharmacies can't tell you why.

**Slide 2 — Why it matters**
The demand is there. The funding is there. The qualification is there.
The ceiling is almost never any of those things.

**Slide 3 — What breaks at scale**
At 300 consultations a month, every manual step compounds.
Booking by phone. Paper consent. Notes in a separate system.
Follow-up from memory.
At 30, someone just about keeps up.
At 300, it collapses.

**Slide 4 — The hidden cost**
It's not just slower. It's unsafe.
Consultations without proper documentation are a governance liability.
Follow-ups that depend on someone remembering are follow-ups that don't happen.

**Slide 5 — The mechanism**
Scaling NMS — or any service — requires three things to be systematic:
Booking that flows straight into the clinical workflow.
Consultations that generate compliant documentation automatically.
Follow-up that is owned by the system, not by the individual.

**Slide 6 — Proof**
Knights Pharmacy moved NMS to central delivery across multiple branches using Titanverse.
One pharmacist. Group-wide visibility. The transformation happened quickly.

**Slide 7 — Takeaway**
300 NMS isn't a staffing problem.
It's a systems problem.
When the infrastructure is right, the number follows.

### Caption
From 30 to 300 NMS consultations a month.

The pharmacies that get there aren't working harder.
They have a different operating model.

Manual booking. Paper consent. Notes in a separate system. Follow-up by memory.

That works at 30. It breaks at 300. Not slowly. Fast.

Scaling any clinical service properly needs three things running automatically: booking into workflow, documentation into compliance, follow-up into the system.

When that infrastructure exists, the ceiling moves.

Six handoffs per consultation. None of them connected.

**Slide 4 — What a connected workflow looks like**
Patient books through the platform. Pre-consultation form arrives before they do.
The pharmacist opens one screen. The pathway is there. The BNF is there. The risk assessment is there.

**Slide 5 — During the consultation**
The pharmacist talks. Notes populate in structured format as the conversation happens.
Nothing is typed during the clinical exchange.
The consultation stays a conversation.

**Slide 6 — After the consultation**
Summary ready. Prescription generated in the same flow. Sent directly to the PMR.
Follow-up task created automatically.
No switching. No duplication. No memory required.

**Slide 7 — Takeaway**
12 minutes of clinical work.
12 minutes total.
That is what a service operation looks like when the infrastructure is right.
