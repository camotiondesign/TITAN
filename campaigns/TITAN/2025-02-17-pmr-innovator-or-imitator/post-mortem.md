## Learnings

### What Worked

- **Positioning messaging:** Content successfully positioned TITAN as innovator vs legacy PMRs as imitators
- **Visual concept worked:** Split lightbulb concept effectively communicated innovator vs imitator differentiation
- **Good engagement:** 7.5% engagement rate and 5.6% CTR indicate strong audience interest
- **Clear differentiation:** Content effectively differentiated innovators (automate, reduce admin, solve inefficiencies) from imitators (react, patch, work around)
- **Reposts indicate value:** 3 reposts suggest content was valuable for sharing with peers
- **Forward-thinking positioning:** Content positioned TITAN as forward-thinking and ahead of competition

### What Didn't Work

- **Limited comments:** Only 1 comment despite 91 engagements suggests content was consumed but didn't spark discussion
- **No discussion engagement:** Discussion prompt didn't generate broader conversation
- **Could add quantified benefits:** Missing specific examples or proof points of innovation

### Audience Insights

- **Positioning content resonates:** Good engagement (7.5%) indicates strong interest in innovation positioning
- **Visual concept works:** Split lightbulb concept effectively communicated differentiation
- **Click-through indicates value:** 5.6% CTR suggests audience found content valuable and worth exploring
- **Reposts indicate value:** 3 reposts suggest content was valuable for sharing
- **Forward-thinking messaging works:** Content positioning TITAN as forward-thinking resonated
