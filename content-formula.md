## The Rules

These exist to stop the patterns that killed the golden era.

### 1. Never repeat a post type back-to-back
Two customer transformation stories in a row = audience fatigue. The whole point of the slots is variety. If a post type lands on consecutive days (even across brands), swap one.

### 2. Rotate customers — minimum 3-week gap per person
If Sagar appears in B2 (Week 2 transformation story), he does not appear again until at least Week 1 of the following month. Build a customer rotation list and track it.

**Current customer roster for rotation:**
- Titan PMR: Sagar, Prab, Rahul, Geoff, Glen, Jeet, Yusuf (BMP), Sunil (Hollywood), Asif (Safy's), Tanzil, Sajid, Riddhesh, Kanav, Paula, Nas (Abel)
- Titanverse: Dervis, Rahul, Glen, Early Adopters (group)

### 3. Format spread across each week
Every week must include at least 2 of the 3 main formats:
- Carousel
- Single image
- Video

Never a full week of one format. The golden era averaged 40% carousel, 30% single image, 30% video.

### 4. At least 2 Tier 1 posts per week (Titan PMR)
Every week needs at least 2 of: Customer Transformation, Industry Stance, or Meme/Relatable. These are the engagement engines. A week of only Tier 2-3 posts will underperform.

### 5. Monthly minimums

**Titan PMR (per month):**
| Type | Minimum | Maximum | Notes |

| Customer Transformation Story | 2 | 3 | THE core content. Non-negotiable. |
| Industry Stance / Controversy | 1 | 2 | Needs genuine news or real opinion. Don't force it. |
| Meme / Relatable | 2 | 3 | No pharmacy page does this well. It's your edge. |
| Customer Quote Card | 2 | 4 | Quick wins. Always have 2-3 pre-made in the bank. |
| Video Testimonial | 1 | 2 | Only publish when the clip is genuinely strong. |
| Reactive / News | 1 | 2 | If nothing's happening, swap for Safety/Awareness. |
| Engagement Question | 1 | 2 | Don't overuse — loses impact. |
| Cultural / Calendar | 0 | 2 | Only when relevant. Eid, Ramadan, Christmas, NHS birthdays. |

**Titanverse (per month):**

| POV / Walkthrough Carousel | 2 | 3 | Show the product doing the thing. |

### 6. The golden era recipe (non-negotiable for Tier 1 posts)

Every Customer Transformation Story must have ALL FOUR:
- ✅ Named person (first name + pharmacy name)
- ✅ Specific numbers (items/month, hours saved, % improvement)
- ✅ Before/after arc (what was broken → what changed → where they are now)
- ✅ Emotional hook in the first 2 lines (something that makes you stop scrolling)

If any ingredient is missing, the post drops from Tier 1 to Tier 2 performance. This is the single biggest lesson from the golden era data.

### 7. Caption voice checklist
Before publishing any caption, check:
- [ ] Written from patient/pharmacist perspective, not brand perspective
- [ ] No product name in the pitch line (hashtag does that work)
- [ ] Bullets say HOW, not just WHAT
- [ ] Reads like a person thinking, not a copywriter performing
- [ ] Emojis used as list markers and section openers (not overloaded)
- [ ] Dash bullets for lists
- [ ] "does not" used sparingly for emphasis weight

### 8. Reactive post protocol
When pharmacy news breaks:
1. It replaces the WEAKEST scheduled post that week (usually Engagement Question or Quote Card)
2. Must go live within 48 hours or don't bother
3. Always take a clear position — "interesting" is not a post
4. Tie it back to what pharmacies can actually do about it

### 9. TitanUp / Campaign override
During campaign periods (e.g., TitanUp June), up to 40% of slots can be campaign content. The other 60% stays formula. Never go 100% campaign — the audience needs variety even during a push.

## Monthly Planning Checklist

Use this at the start of each month to fill the template:

1. **Check the calendar.** Any cultural dates, NHS deadlines, conferences, or campaign milestones this month?
2. **Pick your transformation stories.** Which 2 customers are getting the spotlight? Check the rotation list — they should not have appeared in the last 3 weeks.
3. **Check the news.** Anything in pharmacy worth a stance post? Fill the reactive slot or leave it flexible.
4. **Pick your memes.** What shared pharmacy frustration hasn't been used recently? The audience loves "this is my life" content.
5. **Fill quote cards.** Pull 2-3 strong one-liners from interview transcripts. Pre-design them so they're ready.
6. **Check the format spread.** Across the month: roughly 5 carousels, 4 single images, 4 videos for Titan PMR. Adjust if lopsided.
7. **Write the hooks first.** Before designing anything, write the first 2 lines of every caption. If the hook isn't strong, the post won't land regardless of design.
8. **Score before you publish.** Run each post through the pre-publish scoring framework (scripts/score-post.js). Anything under 30/50 gets reworked.

## What Broke After the Golden Era (for reference)

The decline from Aug 2025 to Jan 2026 happened because:

1. **Customer stories lost specificity.** Generic "we love Titan" replaced "4,000 to 17,000 items."
2. **Memes and relatable content disappeared.** The fun stopped. Feed became a brochure.
3. **Same customers repeated too quickly.** Audience stopped paying attention.
4. **Reactive posts stopped.** No industry commentary = no reason to follow beyond product updates.
5. **Captions got copywriter-y.** Clever tricks replaced genuine insight.
6. **Video quality stayed but caption quality dropped.** Good footage, weak framing.
7. **No monthly plan.** Week-by-week guessing led to format clumps and type repetition.

This formula exists to prevent all seven.

## Blog / Thought Leadership (Monthly)

LinkedIn is the engine. Blog is the depth.

**Target: 2 blog posts per month.** Cam writes these — Claude structures and edits.

Full strategy, topic lists, workflow, and voice rules: `strategy/thought-leadership.md`

### How it integrates with the monthly plan

Each blog post generates 2-3 LinkedIn posts (carousel from the core argument, quote card from a key stat, customer quote card if applicable). These fill existing formula slots — they don't create new ones.

| Monthly step | What happens |
|-------------|-------------|
| Month start | Pick 2 topics from the pillar list (see thought-leadership.md). Rotate pillars quarterly. |
| Week 1-2 | Cam drafts Post 1. Claude edits. Publish. Create LinkedIn derivatives for formula slots. |
| Week 3-4 | Cam drafts Post 2. Claude edits. Publish. Create LinkedIn derivatives for formula slots. |

### The four pillars (rotate 2 per month)

1. **Industry Lens** — what's happening in pharmacy and what it means for owners
2. **Operational Thinking** — how modern pharmacies actually run (from 1,000+ implementations)
3. **AI in Pharmacy** — where AI is going in community pharmacy, not just what Titan AI does
4. **Services & Clinical Practice** — how pharmacy is shifting from dispensing to clinical delivery

### Monthly planning addition

Add to the monthly planning checklist:
- [ ] Pick 2 blog topics from different pillars
- [ ] Check which LinkedIn slots the blog derivatives will fill (usually Industry Stance or Thought Leadership slots)
- [ ] Schedule blog publish dates for weeks 2 and 4
