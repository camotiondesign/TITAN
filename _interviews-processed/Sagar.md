- Hours saved: not stated numerically; "80% of my time goes into services"

- Services added: private travel clinic, ear suction, BP monitoring (NHS), contraception (NHS), weight loss (private), more planned; recently completed refit with 2 consultation rooms
- Dispensing model: independent (ex-Boots); first Titan + Meditech robot integration installation (6 months ago at time of interview)

## The Before State
Sagar was previously at Lloyds Pharmacy as a manager. He first saw Titan at the Pharmacy Show before he owned a pharmacy. When he bought Brighton Hill (ex-Boots, 4,000 items/month), he came from RX Web and other systems that were nothing like Titan. "There was nothing out there like Titan PMR at the time." He knew from day one that Titan was his only choice.

## The Tipping Point
"From day one, I had no doubt about it. Titan was the PMR I had to go for." He had seen it at the Pharmacy Show and it was the first thing he thought of when planning his own pharmacy. No alternative was considered.

## What Changed
Titan AI handles clinical checks in the morning, freeing Sagar to run services from ~10–11am onwards. The workflow model: clinical checks first thing → services from mid-morning → brief dispensary check mid-afternoon → final check near close. 80% of his day is now services. Prescriptions are tracked in real time on the Kanban board, so the counter team always knows exactly where Mrs. Smith's prescription is. Text notifications for patients are fully automated — no manual effort. The Meditech robot integration (first in the UK for Titan+Meditech) was described as going "perfectly smooth from day one." It has "significantly increased efficiency" in dispensing, with seconds saved per prescription adding up across 17,000 items/month.

## The After State
Brighton Hill grew from 4,000 to 17,000 items/month in 4 years — a 4x increase Sagar attributes significantly to Titan. He now runs a full private services suite including weight loss, travel, earwax, and has a fully refitted pharmacy with 2 consultation rooms specifically designed for future growth. He is the first Titan+Meditech robot installation. He's planning IP accreditation for more private services. He's described as a "technology guy" who is now exploring AI technologies with an eye to designing pharmacy-specific products.

## Best Direct Quotes
- "From day one, I had no doubt about it. Titan was the PMR which I had to go for."
- "When I started the pharmacy, we started with 4,000 items, and in just about four years now, we're doing around 17,000 items. So since the beginning of owning the pharmacy, from day one I had Titan."
- "My core model of the pharmacy is the services. That's the future."
- "80% of my time goes into services. I spend [only] an hour in the dispensary."
- "I was the first Titan Meditech robot installation, and in my opinion, it went perfectly smooth from day one."
- "Titan PMR will be the one to go for for my expanding pharmacies as well."
- "Always try to inform patients — and they're very like, in awe — 'Oh, we do provide all these services!'"

## Broader Vision / Ambition
Sagar plans to own several more pharmacies in his area. He will roll out Titan (and evaluate Meditech robots per volume) in each new acquisition. He wants to bring IP on board for the pharmacy to drive private services further, and is spending increasing time understanding AI technologies — potentially designing pharmacy-specific AI products. He sees services as the permanent core model, not dispensing.
