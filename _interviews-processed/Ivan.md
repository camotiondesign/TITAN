## The Before State
Ivan is in a staff/supervisor role managing stock ordering at Rexel Chemist. Before Titan (day two of implementation at time of recording), Owings visibility was fragmented — it fell to one person (Ivan) to track and chase, with no shared team visibility.

## The Tipping Point
Not a structured case study — day-two on-site testimonial. Ivan immediately identifies the Owings board as a standout feature.

## What Changed
The Owings board visibility means the whole team can see where outstanding items are and update statuses — not just Ivan. Batch flow allows 8 prescriptions to be picked in one pass, checked quickly, and prepared for delivery or collection faster. Ivan found prescription location/visibility especially useful: being able to see where each prescription is in the workflow at a glance.

## The After State
Ivan is positive and does not want to return to the old system after just two days. The team is functioning more smoothly on stock visibility and workflow transparency.

## Best Direct Quotes
- "For me, because I manage the stock ordering, it's the visibility of all the Owings on one page and seeing where they are, and being able to update the status for each item so that the entire team can see where it's coming from. So it's not just me."
- "Being able to pick, you know, eight prescriptions all in one go, and then assign them to their own baskets, and then getting those checked into the system quickly and then bagged quickly, and then out for deliveries or out to the patients."
- "It's been really good seeing the visibility of where each prescription is in each stage."
