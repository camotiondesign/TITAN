## Quick Stats
- Items per month (before): ~6,000–6,500 (where she is now, also with Titan)
- Items per month (after): same volume; the story is time saved, not volume grown
- Hours saved: pharmacist saves ~10 hours/week; "4 hours a day at least" not checking baskets; ~4–5 hours per day saved as a team in total
- Staff: 2 full-time dispensers/ACDs, 1 full-time post office, 1 trainee dispenser (30hrs/week); could have needed 1 extra full-timer without Titan
- Services added: Earwax removal, BP checks, ABPM, private weight management (18 active clients, growing), travel, NMS (5–6/month → 60–65/month), flu/COVID clinics; "2–3 private consultations a day"
- Dispensing model: independent (Pritee owns; previously owned 3 pharmacies, now focused on 1; pharmacy has a co-located post office)

## The Before State
Pritee was superintendent at Harlots for 10+ years (locum there 20+ years ago, bought it when the previous owner retired). Before Titan, she ran on ProScript. A dedicated middle bench was always full of baskets waiting for the pharmacist to check — "even as quickly as you might be checking them, there's more and more baskets being added." End-of-month claiming was a full day or two of manually scanning tokens — a task so mundane staff would leave it for her. She was running three pharmacies, and without Titan she couldn't oversee them without physically visiting each one. Services (BP checks, NMS) were severely limited: 10–15 BP checks/month vs 60–70 now; 5–6 NMS/month vs 60–65 now.

## The Tipping Point
Pritee wasn't primarily trying to cut staff when she moved to Titan — she was trying to free existing staff to do more. "I was looking at ways of working more efficiently, to free the staff up to be able to do other jobs, like helping with BP checks and things." The realisation that the technology could act as a second pair of eyes (clinically) while also removing the admin burden of checking every basket was the core shift.

## What Changed
Titan's barcode scanning removed the requirement for Pritee to physically check most prescriptions — only split packs and CDs go to her for accuracy checking. Titan AI handles 50–60% of clinical checks (sometimes more). End-of-month claims are sent on a daily basis, completed by midday on the 1st. Repeat flow automates dosset patient prescriptions for non-manual repeat ordering. NMS flagging catches patients she would have missed. She can view her dispensary remotely, monitoring process compliance from home. The whole team — now freed from dispensing — books patients in, helps with flu clinics, does BP checks, earwax removal, ABPM.

## The After State
NMS: 5–6 → 60–65/month. BP checks: 10–15 → 60–70/month. Private consultations: 1/month → 2–3/day. Weight management: 1 client → 18 active clients, 90% word-of-mouth. The staff are upskilled and fulfilled rather than grinding through baskets all day. Three staff members have come forward with mental health crises at the pharmacy — Pritee credits the human touch being available (freed from dispensing) as the reason they felt safe to do so. Three near-misses per month (down dramatically); only one actual error in 3 years.

## Best Direct Quotes
- "Typically, that bench would be full of baskets waiting to be checked. Even as quickly as you might be checking them, there's more and more baskets being added."
- "I think the worst thing we had to do with ProScript is where we would be printing off the tokens for all prescriptions we dispensed, and then using those tokens once we handed out to scan, to claim, which could take up a whole day, two days at the end of the month."
- "I would probably be saving on a weekly basis I think definitely about 10 hours a week on time spent just checking, possibly more."
- "We've gone from like five or six NMS a month to hitting 60–65 a month."
- "I've gone from one private consultation a month to seeing two or three a day. And that also means where I'm free, I'm able to give that patient my time in a less stressful manner, so that your reputation goes up."
- "I've had three people with mental health issues that have talked about suicide to us — and all I can say is I'm grateful that they felt they could come to any one of us in the team... Technology allows you to free your people up to be there for the community."
- "If you treat our patients the way we would want our family members to be treated, then you're never going to go wrong."
- "I've saved money on a second pharmacist. If I were to do the same volume of work today without Titan, I definitely would need a second pharmacist."

## Broader Vision / Ambition
Pritee wants to complete her IP qualification as her next personal development step. She is developing a dispenser into a technician. She is exploring blood tests as a new private service. Her broader philosophy: technology frees people to be human — the dispensary runs efficiently so the team can focus on the irreplaceable human moments that pharmacy uniquely enables in the community.
