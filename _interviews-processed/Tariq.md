## Core Themes of the Talk

**1. Pharmacy's complacency trap**
The "heyday" of pharmacy — opening near a GP surgery and waiting for scripts — created complacency. The profession didn't invest in its image or future. This complacency shaped how MPs, civil servants, and policymakers perceived pharmacy: as shopkeepers, not healthcare professionals. That perception led directly to the ongoing funding cuts starting 2005.

**2. The rhetoric vs reality gap**
NHS and government consistently promise pharmacy a bright future and dangle services, but the implementation never matches. Examples: MUR abolished, NMS outsourcing blocked, Pharmacy First rollout hampered by accreditation. "The delivery of it does not match that rhetoric."

**3. The shift to private services**
Titan surveyed ~95 customers. The data: majority are skeptical of the 10-year plan; 59% want to grow NHS services but barriers are GPS and funding; desire to grow private services is "off the scale." The real opportunity for pharmacy now is private services — "we have to take control back into our own hands."

**4. Six strategic principles for pharmacy's future**
- Own dispensing at 100% but have 0% involvement (use technology to remove pharmacist from routine dispensing)
- Use NHS patient data as the audience for private services
- Fix the profession's image (Chupa Chups on counters = shopkeeper image; consultation rooms = professional image)
- Create a new, differentiated offering (not competing with GPs but complementing them)
- Build trust through real compliance and quality, not tick-boxes
- Be efficient — serve patients better than GPs through technology-enabled slick operations

**5. Titanverse as the vehicle**
Titanverse is described as the "complete solution for all services" — pharmacy-only, AI-scribing consultations (not typing while facing patients), auto-generating templates from scratch, AI trained on BNF and NATHNAC not generic AI, direct prescribing integration with Titan, CRM for marketing NHS patients into private services, SOP compliance with AI-generated assessments.

## Best Direct Quotes
- "We became complacent. We became dependent on the NHS. And the whole theme of what we did was based on that. We relaxed. But what happened is we didn't really focus on our image and our future."
- "The image we created as a profession in the past has led to the present that we have today. So if you are coming to a shop refit, think about your brand."
- "For most of us who are running a pharmacy business, dispensing is not viable on its own. It's been squeezed so much."
- "The desire to grow private services — that was really off the scale. There is a real desire for pharmacists now to grow their private services, because there's a belief that we have to take control back into our own hands."
- "We want to be an industry where we own the dispensing process — we are 100% in control, but we have 0% involvement."
- "Technology is not just something where you record your services. Technology is an enabler. Technology is the vehicle that will help us get to the destination."
- "My strap line for pharmacy would be: be more dentist."
- "We want to enable pharmacists to offer affordable healthcare to patients. That is a future that we want to get to, but in order to get to that, we need a vehicle. And technology is that vehicle."
