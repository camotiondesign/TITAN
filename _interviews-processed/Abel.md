## The Before State
Abel's pharmacy was reaching a point where clinical work and patient-facing services were being seriously compromised by the volume of dispensing work. There was no way to separate pharmacist time from the physical checking of prescriptions.

## The Tipping Point
"We chose Titan because it was getting to a stage where our clinical work, through our services, was being really compromised by the level of dispensing work that we had, so we had to find a different way of working."

## What Changed
The key feature for Abel was the ability to partially complete a prescription check — releasing it to the next step without having to personally follow the item start to finish. This let customers get what they need and leave while Abel focused on complex patient consultations.

## The After State
Abel was able to hold four or five long patient consultations in a row without having to worry about prescriptions being checked. The interview took place during Titan training at Vexil Chemist, so full before/after stats are not yet available.

## Best Direct Quotes
- "It was getting to a stage where our clinical work, through our services, was being really compromised by the level of dispensing work that we had, so we had to find a different way of working."
- "The other day, I was able to have four or five long consultations with patients without having to worry about prescriptions being checked."
- "The ability to be able to check a prescription allows somebody else to complete it without having to completely bag it and finish it — we start to finish — that allows our customers to get what they need and leave, whilst allowing me to focus on patients with more complex needs."

## Broader Vision / Ambition
Abel clearly sees Titan as the vehicle to grow clinical services. The pharmacy is in early implementation at the time of recording, so specific growth targets are not yet stated.
