## The Before State
Before Titan, Jeet's pharmacies used a traditional CTS (checking technician system) but were "always firefighting" because they didn't know where prescriptions were. Even with CTS, the pharmacist had to check a mountain of baskets. Jeet was personally staying in branches until 11pm to clear work, and hadn't seen his young daughter grow up for a year: "By the time I go home, she was off to bed. And by the time I moved out in the morning, she was still asleep." Stock management errors caused repeated unnecessary reordering — boxes of £100 drugs being reordered when they were already on the shelf. One branch had ~1,000 prescriptions with 2 dispensers and no way for a well-staffed branch 50 miles away to help.

## The Tipping Point
The realization that multiple branches were operating in isolation — one overwhelmed, one underutilised — with no way to share capacity. "I had to make a decision to say, how are we going to be more efficient so that we are more profitable." This led to the creation of the hub, and then the discovery of peer-to-peer functionality in Titan which made traditional hub-and-spoke feel like only one half of the solution.

## What Changed
The hub-and-spoke model built on Titan PMR allows spoke branches to send prescriptions to the hub for dispensing while focusing entirely on clinical services. Dispensers at the hub went from 12–15 items/hour to 40–45 items/hour (4x efficiency) because they have no interruptions. Stock holding at branch level reduced by 45%. Peer-to-peer functionality allows any branch to send prescriptions to any other branch, not just the hub — creating even more flexible capacity management. Titan AI handles ~50% of clinical checking at spoke level. The hub operates with a robot (presumably Meditech). Role-based functionality in Titan allows training dispensers in carefully staged steps.

## The After State
Jeet now leaves at a normal time and has been "managing to become a dad." The hub is running, spokes are converting into clinical service centres with consultation rooms replacing dispensing shelves. Technicians are doing PGD services (UTI etc.), level-2 dispensers are training for vaccinations, pharmacists are doing private services. The vision: "Each of these pharmacies are never going to be called pharmacies. They're all going to be classed as a health clinic." 45% reduction in branch stock holding. Totes going from 20 per day down to 2. Peer-to-peer functionality is a "brand new tool" Jeet sees as potentially even more powerful than traditional hub-and-spoke.

## Best Direct Quotes
- "Before we moved to Titan, I was spending my time in the branches till about 11 at night in the trying to clear the floor... I had a young daughter who I don't think I've seen her grow that one year, because by the time I go home, she was off to bed."
- "Freeing up pharmacist time can do that to make your dad as well."
- "Dispensing should be the last thing on everybody's head, and that's what Titan has enabled us to do."
- "We've reduced about 45% of stock holding capacity at branch level at the minute by using peer to peer."
- "We're now in a position where people used to shy away to buy pharmacies. We are now thinking we can get more pharmacies."
- "The dispensers were doing about 12 to 15 items an hour. What we have now done... we're looking at about 40 to 45 items an hour — four times more efficient."
- "This was a life changer — as a pharmacist, as a contractor, as a dad, as a husband. We've kept ourselves together, and that's what Titan has done for us."

## Broader Vision / Ambition
Jeet's vision is for all spoke pharmacies to become "health clinics" — patients come for advice, clinical services, and pick up prescriptions that arrive ready on the shelf. Dispensing is entirely handled by the hub. He is now considering more pharmacy acquisitions because Titan (and the hub model) has made multi-site expansion feel manageable rather than terrifying. Peer-to-peer is the next major strategic tool.
