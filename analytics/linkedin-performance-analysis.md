## Methodology: Quality Engagement Score (QES)

Standard LinkedIn engagement rate is misleading for carousels and multi-image posts because carousel slide swipes register as clicks, inflating CTR and ER to 50–110% — numbers that aren't comparable to single images or video.

**QES formula:**

```
QES = (Reactions + Comments × 3 + Reposts × 2) ÷ Impressions × 1,000
```

| Signal | Weight | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Reactions | 1 | Baseline engagement |
| Comments | 3 | High-intent, hardest to get |
| Reposts | 2 | Public advocacy / amplification |

- Normalised per 1,000 impressions — removes reach bias
- Clicks/CTR entirely excluded — removes carousel inflation
- Boosted posts excluded from rankings (paid impressions distort the denominator)
- Minimum threshold: 100 impressions

### What Works

#### 1. Live event content — by far the highest ceiling
The Pharmacy Show produced the single best week in Titan's content history. The announcement video (54.1 QES) beat everything else — 15 comments, 55 reactions on under 2,000 impressions. The album, recap montage, Zad's street interviews, Tariq's keynote — all elite. The common thread: real energy, real people, unpredictable reactions, a sense of something happening. It creates genuine community participation, not just consumption.

#### 2. Industry controversy and policy flashpoints
Stop nomination ping-pong (26.5 QES, 14 reposts) punched well above its weight because it took a stance on an issue people care about. Mounjaro pricing changes and pharmacy contract changes follow the same pattern — posts that name a real threat and explain it clearly. These generate reposts because people forward them to colleagues. The repost signal is the key here: it means the post has utility beyond the feed.

#### 3. Customer transformation stories with specific numbers
Sagar 4x growth, BMP case study video, Puri Health Hub — the common denominator is specificity. "4x items", "170,000 items a month", "one pilot site turned into one a month." Vague transformation stories don't land. Named customers with real numbers do.

#### 4. Meme and cultural content
Christmas carousel (81.6% raw ER), 2025 pharmacy memes (85.6% raw ER) — yes, carousel-inflated, but even stripping that out they drove strong reactions because pharmacy humour is a genuine community identity signal. People tag colleagues. It builds warmth and reach at the same time.

#### 5. Safety topics with a hook
GTIN barcode safety hit 7,338 organic impressions with 65 reactions and 12 reposts. Safety topics with a counter-intuitive hook ("Can you spot the difference?") travel further than most — the repost count signals the content is being shared as useful information.

### What Doesn't Work

#### 1. Boosted posts — weak organic signal
Every boosted post shows the same pattern: massive impressions, terrible engagement. Scale Smarter had 72,479 impressions and 0.1% engagement. Asif Safy's Chemist longform video had 13,615 impressions and 1.1% engagement. The content doesn't resonate with the cold audience being served. Paid reach without organic pull-through is just burning money on impressions.

#### 2. Generic product feature posts
Posts that lead with "Titan does X" without a customer name, a specific outcome, or an industry problem consistently underperform. "Partial systems create full-scale problems" (635 impressions, 4.1% ER). "Pharmacy, but smarter" (6,491 impressions boosted, 3.1% organic ER). The audience doesn't care about the feature — they care about the outcome.

#### 3. HOD short-form clips in isolation
The recent Head of Dispensing video series has decent Notionsocial views but the individual clips without context or a strong hook don't build on each other. "Only about 14–15% of items need a pharmacist check" is a valid insight — but talking head clips without a visual hook, data graphic, or narrative setup don't stop the scroll.

#### 4. Duplicate posting (Notionsocial + organic)
Multiple posts in Jan–Feb 2026 appear twice — once via Notionsocial (views/likes/shares format) and once organically (impressions/engagement format) with near-identical captions. This dilutes the audience rather than compounding performance.

### Do More Of

- **Event content clusters.** Pharmacy Show worked because it was a week-long story arc — announcement, build-up, live day one, live day two, recap montage, individual testimonial clips. Build the same structure around any future events, launches, or industry moments.
- **Industry stance posts.** One per fortnight minimum. Name a real problem, take a clear position, explain the stakes. These drive reposts — your highest-value signal.
- **Named customer stories with hard numbers.** One proper case study carousel per month. Lead with the number in the first line: "97% fewer near misses." "4x items in 4 years." "20 hours freed every week."
- **Meme/cultural content.** Once a month. Pharmacy identity humour builds goodwill and reach simultaneously. It doesn't require big production.
- **Controversy hooks on single images.** GTIN barcode safety style — a provocative question or visual with a counter-intuitive insight that earns shares.

### Do Less Of

- **Boosting generic brand awareness posts.** If you're going to boost, boost posts that already have organic traction. Amplify what's working — don't try to brute-force posts that have no organic pull.
- **Product feature posts without a human.** Always anchor to someone real — a customer name, a specific outcome, an industry problem.
- **Short clips without context.** Single talking-head clips without a graphic hook, data point, or narrative setup underperform against structured carousel or full case study formats.

Posts like "one-place-all-in-flow-loop-video" and "prescribing-built-in-consultation-loop-video" (0% engagement, 0 clicks) show that product demo loops without a compelling hook or narrative frame don't hold attention. The product might be impressive but a short silent loop doesn't communicate it.
