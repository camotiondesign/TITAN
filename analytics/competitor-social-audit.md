# Social Media Audit: SaaS Inspiration + Competitor Analysis

**Date:** 2026-04-07
**Purpose:** Identify what leading SaaS brands do well on LinkedIn, audit pharmacy competitors, and extract actionable tactics for Titan PMR and Titanverse.

---

## Part 1: SaaS Inspiration Audit

### ClickUp — The Gold Standard for B2B Meme Marketing

**What they did:** Hired Melissa Rosenthal (ex-BuzzFeed, ex-Cheddar) as CCO and allocated a significant portion of their Series A to building an in-house creative agency. They applied B2C viral content tactics to B2B SaaS marketing.

**Key tactics:**
- **Meme-driven storytelling**: Short-form skits and memes that address real pain points project managers face — but wrapped in humor
- **Competitor trolling**: Tapped into shared frustrations with competitor tools (Monday.com, Asana) using humor rather than feature comparison
- **Creator-first approach**: TikTok/Instagram Reels-style videos, meme templates, highly shareable "work pain" content
- **Cross-platform distribution**: Same content adapted for TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn, Reddit — not platform-native creation for each

**Why it works:** Every piece of content ties back to a real problem their ICP faces. The humor opens the door, the product is the punchline. They don't pitch features — they make you laugh at the problem, then present the solution.

**Titan takeaway:** Pharmacy has ENDLESS relatable pain points (5 systems, Friday EPS crashes, locum handover chaos, the 4:55pm prescription rush). Nobody in pharmacy is making this funny. Titan can own this.

### Semrush — Expert-Led Authority

**What they do:**
- Heavy on educational content — guides, data reports, how-tos
- Position themselves as the definitive expert in their space
- Use their own tools to demonstrate value (meta-marketing)
- Weekly content drives 2x audience engagement

**Titan takeaway:** The analytics/data angle works for Titanverse specifically. "We ran the numbers on pharmacy service delivery" style posts that use real data to establish authority.

### Hootsuite — Scale Through Video + Conversation

**Key stats:**
- Grew LinkedIn from 0 to 511,000 followers
- Video views up 36% YoY on LinkedIn
- Comments up 37% YoY — algorithm rewards conversation
- Video creation growing 2x faster than all other post types

**What they preach (and practice):**
- Algorithm now rewards human expertise and originality over generic content
- Shallow "post and pray" is dead — active participation wins
- Video from industry leaders gets algorithmic priority
- Being identified as a niche expert = wider content distribution

**Titan takeaway:** Titan's video content (customer interviews, Head Office Days) already outperforms. Double down. The algorithm literally favours what Titan already has — real humans, real expertise, real stories.

## Part 2: B2B Meme Formats That Work

Based on research across 27+ B2B meme examples and leading SaaS brands:

### Format 1: "Nobody: ..."
> Nobody:
> The dispensary at 4:55pm on a Friday: [chaos image]

### Format 2: "POV: [universal frustration]"
> POV: You're logging into your fifth system before 10am
*(Already using this for Titanverse — scale it to Titan PMR too)*

### Format 3: "How it started / How it's going"
> How it started: "We'll just use a spreadsheet for now"
> How it's going: [photo of 47-tab Excel monster]

### Format 4: "Me: ... Also me:"
> Me: "We need to modernise our pharmacy"
> Also me: *prints the EPS token and walks it to the dispensary*

### Format 5: Competitor/category trolling (subtle)
> "When your PMR vendor says 'we'll get back to you' about that integration"
> [waiting skeleton meme]

### Format 6: Industry-specific inside jokes
> "Tell me you work in pharmacy without telling me you work in pharmacy"
> [specific pharmacy moment everyone recognises]

### The Rule: Memes as appetiser, value as main course
Pair humor with insight. The meme grabs attention, then follow it with a stat, a tip, or a product CTA. Four categories that consistently perform:
1. **Product memes** — your problem vs our solution
2. **Profession memes** — pharmacist-specific daily life
3. **Trending memes** — adapt current formats to pharmacy
4. **Original brand memes** — create your own recurring formats

## Part 3: Competitor LinkedIn Audit

### Cegedim Rx (Pharmacy Manager)
- **Followers:** ~6,316 (Cegedim Healthcare Solutions UK)
- **Market share:** ~2,500 pharmacies
- **Tone:** Corporate, institutional, formal
- **Content:** Product updates, partnership announcements, compliance news
- **Weakness:** Zero personality. No human stories. No humor. Feels like a press release feed.
- **Titan advantage:** Titan's human-first storytelling is lightyears ahead

### RxWeb (part of Clanwilliam)
- **Presence:** LinkedIn showcase page
- **Positioning:** "UK's only web-based PMR" — cloud-first messaging
- **Content:** Feature-focused, technical specs, remote access benefits
- **Weakness:** Small presence, no community engagement, no customer stories
- **Titan advantage:** RxWeb talks about features. Titan talks about people.

### Positive Solutions (Analyst PMR)
- **Content:** System updates, contractual compliance
- **Tone:** Dry, operational
- **Weakness:** Purely functional content — "we updated the system to meet X requirement"
- **Titan advantage:** Positive Solutions makes pharmacy sound boring. Titan makes it human.

### Pharmacy2U
- **Followers:** 12,525
- **Positioning:** "UK's largest online pharmacy" — 750,000+ patients
- **Content:** Service announcements, NHS partnerships, hiring
- **Strength:** Large audience, consumer-facing brand recognition
- **Weakness:** B2C focused, not speaking to pharmacy owners/professionals
- **Titanverse advantage:** Pharmacy2U talks TO patients. Titanverse talks TO pharmacy leaders about the future.

### Phlo
- **Positioning:** "Digital Pharmacy of the future"
- **Content:** Digital services, job postings, awareness campaigns
- **Strength:** 5,000+ Trustpilot reviews (4.8/5), modern brand feel
- **Weakness:** Still relatively small, content is functional not inspiring
- **Titanverse advantage:** Phlo has the brand feel but not the thought leadership depth

## Part 4: Competitive Positioning

### Where Titan Already Wins
1. **Human stories** — No competitor features named customers with emotional depth. Titan has Jeet, Prab, Geoff, Sajid, Tanzil. Nobody else comes close.
2. **Video content** — Competitors barely use video. Titan has customer interviews, Head Office Days, documentary-style content.
3. **Design quality** — The Titan/Titanverse visual identity is premium. Competitors look like IT companies from 2015.
4. **Community building** — TitanUp events, pharmacy visits, BTS content. No competitor does this.

### The Gap Nobody Is Filling
**NOBODY in UK pharmacy is doing memes, humor, or relatable content on LinkedIn.**

Every competitor is corporate. Every competitor is formal. Every competitor talks about features and compliance.

This is the biggest opportunity: **Titan can be the ClickUp of pharmacy software.** Make pharmacists laugh. Make them feel seen. Make them share.

### Gaps to Close
1. **Posting frequency** — Competitors post rarely (1-2x/week max). Titan's 3.3/week is already ahead, but consistency matters.
2. **Data-led authority** — Semrush's model of using their own data works for Titanverse. "We analysed 500 pharmacy workflows" type content.
3. **Cross-platform** — Most competitor content is LinkedIn-only. Expanding to TikTok (which Titan does) is a genuine advantage.

## Part 5: Actionable Recommendations

### For Titan PMR

1. **Monthly meme post**: Take a relatable pharmacy pain point and wrap it in a trending meme format. The dispensary at closing time. The locum handover. The "temporary" workaround that's been running for 3 years.

2. **"Tell me you work in pharmacy" series**: User-generated-style posts that invite pharmacists to share their daily reality. Builds community and engagement.

3. **Before/After with real numbers**: "Before Titan: 11pm in the branches. After Titan: I managed to become a dad." — already doing this well. Make it a recurring format.

4. **Feature reveals as memes**: Don't announce "New integration with X". Instead: "When your PMR vendor says they integrate with your robot... and they actually do" [surprised Pikachu]. Feature is the punchline, not the headline.

5. **Friday pharmacy memes**: A recurring weekly format. Every Friday, one meme that pharmacists can relate to. Low effort to produce, high shareability, builds habit.

### For Titanverse

1. **"POV: You run a pharmacy in 2026" series**: Already planned. Scale it. Monthly minimum.

2. **Data authority posts**: "We looked at how 100 pharmacies deliver services. Here's what separates the top 10%." Use Titanverse's data advantage.

3. **Industry hot takes**: When news breaks (DSPT changes, funding announcements, Pharmacy First updates), be first with a Titanverse perspective. The Monday news scan feeds this.

4. **Founder/leadership voice**: Dervis, Sajid, Rahul as personal brand amplifiers. Their posts get shared further than the brand page.

5. **Contrarian carousels**: "5 things pharmacy owners believe about digital transformation that are completely wrong." Challenge conventional thinking.

### 5 Specific Post Ideas (Ready to Brief)

1. **TITAN MEME**: "Nobody: ... The pharmacy team when the PMR goes down at 4:30pm:" + image of chaos. Caption pivots to uptime/reliability.

2. **TITAN MEME**: "How it started: 'We'll just track repeats manually for now.' How it's going:" + image of drowning in paper. Caption pivots to Repeat Flow.

3. **TITANVERSE POV**: "POV: You open your pharmacy's 'digital strategy' document and it's a Word file from 2019 with 3 bullet points." Carousel that builds the absurdity.

4. **TITAN RELATABLE**: "Tell me you're a pharmacist without telling me you're a pharmacist. We'll go first: You've explained 'take with food' 47 times today and it's only 2pm."

5. **TITANVERSE HOT TAKE**: "Unpopular opinion: Most pharmacy software wasn't built for pharmacy. It was built for compliance. There's a difference." Stance post that sparks debate.
